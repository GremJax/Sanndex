# Sanndex
A public information repository that assigns a reputation score to internet news providers on accountability, trust, and sensationalism.

## Scoring system:
The Sanndex rating system is universal and unambiguous, with peer review and report referencing to ensure no ratings are biased, influenced, or misrepresentative beyond reasonable doubt. The metric consists of 6 scoring fields rated 1 to 100, with a rubric style grouping system. The total score is the average across all scores. Proper wording and scoring must be further iterated upon for an impartial grading scheme.

### Accuracy
Is your information factual? Does your information contain errors or unmarked speculation?

- 80-100: Information is fully accurate. All speculation is sufficiently labeled. 
- 60-80: Information is mostly accurate. Most speculation is sufficiently labeled. Some innacuracies may be present.
- 40-60: Information is sometimes accurate. Speculation is insufficiently labeled. Information is usually inaccurate and requires supplimentary examination.
- 0-40: Information is not accurate. Speculation is unlabeled and presented as whole truth. Deliberate lies may be present.

### Transparency
How is your content created? Are you consistent with dates, authorship, and funding?

- 80-100: All contributers are properly creditted. All content has publish dates and appropriate method description. Funding contributions are publicly stated or otherwise easily accessible.
- 60-80: Most contributers are properly creditted. Most content usually has publish dates and appropriate method description. Funding contributions are mildly disclosed or accessible with some difficulty.
- 40-60: Some contributers are properly creditted, while others are ignored. Content occasionally has publish dates and appropriate method description. Funding contributions are privately disclosed and inaccessable to the public, but still apparent from public statements.
- 0-40: Almost no contributers are properly creditted. Content does not have publish dates or appropriate method description. Funding contributions are entirely secret and it is impossible to determine how funding is achieved.

### Integrity
Do you correct your mistakes? Do you hold yourself to a high standard of accountability?

- 80-100: All mistakes are corrected swiftly and publicly. Information standards are public and followed.
- 60-80: Most mistakes are corrected swiftly and publicly; some are not corrected or corrected secretly. Information standards are weak and sometimes ignored.
- 40-60: Some mistakes are corrected publicly; most are not corrected or corrected secretly. Information standards are private or usually ignored.
- 0-40: No mistakes are corrected publicly; all are not corrected or corrected secretly. No information standards.

### Honesty
Do you tell the truth to your audience? Is your information misconstrued or sensationalist?

- 80-100: Information is fully neutral. No information is withheld or cherry picked. All political bias is disclosed and apparent. No information is sensationalized or exaggerated.
- 60-80: Information is usually neutral. Some information may be withheld or cherry picked. Political bias is present and may be portrayed as neutral. Some information may be sensationalized or exaggerated.
- 40-60: Information is sometimes neutral. Information is often withheld or cherry picked. Political bias is present and usually portrayed as neutral. Information is often sensationalized or exaggerated.
- 0-40: Information is never neutral. Information is almost entirely withheld or cherry picked. Political bias is pervasive and presented as whole truth. Information is almost entirely sensationalized and exaggerated. Deliberately intends to misinform for an alterior end.

### Authenticity
Are you who you say you are? Is your content AI generated and presented as real?

- 80-100: All non-obvious use of artificial intelligence is publicly creditted and watermarked. AI may be present in workflows without appearing in the final product.
- 60-80: Most non-obvious use of artificial intelligence is publicly creditted and watermarked. AI generated content may be present in the final product without proper annotations.
- 40-60: Some non-obvious use of artificial intelligence is publicly creditted and watermarked. AI generated content may be expected in the final product without proper annotations.
- 0-40: All use of artificial intelligence is not publicly creditted and watermarked. Final product may entirely consist of AI generated content portrayed as whole truth.

### Credibility
Where does your information come from? Do you cite sources, experts, and true accounts?

- 80-100: All sources are properly cited. All involved surveys are unbiased and comprehensive. All cited experts are credible and relevant.
- 60-80: Most sources are properly cited. Most surveys are unbiased and comprehensive. Most cited experts are credible and relevant. Sources may be irrelevant or otherwise unreliable.
- 40-60: Some sources are properly cited. Some surveys are unbiased and comprehensive. Some cited experts are credible and relevant. Sources are usually irrelevant or otherwise unreliable.
- 0-40: No sources are properly cited. Involved surveys are biased and unreliable. Cited experts are unreliable and irrelevant. Information may be entirely fabricated.
